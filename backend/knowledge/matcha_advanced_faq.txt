Q: How does the "Shading Process" change the chemical DNA of tea?
A: Shading the plants for over 21 days is a form of "stress management" that fundamentally alters the leaf's chemistry, preventing the conversion of L-theanine into bitter catechins. Blocking up to 95% of sunlight forces the plant to overproduce chlorophyll and amino acids to survive. This results in a final powder with 10x more L-theanine than sun-grown green tea. This physical intervention is what elevates matcha from a simple beverage to a pharmacological tool for inducing "alpha-wave" relaxation in the human brain.
