Barista Guide — NAKAI Matcha

This guide is for baristas and cafe partners serving NAKAI matcha.

Preparing Matcha Drinks Consistently:

Matcha Base (for all drinks):
- Always sift matcha through a fine-mesh strainer before use. This prevents clumps and ensures a smooth drink.
- Use 2g of matcha per serving (about 2 chashaku scoops or 1 level teaspoon).
- Water temperature must be 75-80°C (167-176°F). Never use boiling water — it makes matcha bitter and astringent.
- Whisk vigorously in an M or W pattern for 10-15 seconds to create a smooth, frothy base.

Hot Matcha Latte (Most Popular):
1. Sift 2g matcha into a cup or pitcher.
2. Add 30ml hot water (75-80°C).
3. Whisk until no clumps remain and a thin layer of foam appears.
4. Steam milk to 65-70°C (149-158°F) — do not overheat.
5. Pour steamed milk over the matcha base.
6. For latte art, pour from a slight height, then bring the pitcher closer to create your pattern.

Iced Matcha Latte:
1. Sift 2g matcha into a glass or shaker.
2. Add 30ml hot water (75-80°C) and whisk until smooth.
3. Fill glass with ice.
4. Pour 200ml cold milk over the ice.
5. Stir gently or shake to combine.

Milk Pairing Guide:
- Oat milk: Best overall pairing with matcha. Creamy, slightly sweet, enhances umami.
- Whole milk: Classic choice, rich and smooth. Great for traditional lattes.
- Almond milk: Light and nutty, pairs well for a lighter drink.
- Soy milk: Creamy texture, neutral flavor, good for hot lattes.
- Coconut milk: Tropical notes, works well in iced drinks.

Common Mistakes and Solutions:

Problem: Matcha tastes bitter.
Solution: Water is too hot. Use 75-80°C, never boiling. Also check that the matcha is fresh (within 2-4 weeks of opening).

Problem: Matcha is clumpy.
Solution: Always sift before use. Whisk more vigorously. Make sure to dissolve in a small amount of hot water before adding milk.

Problem: Matcha settles to the bottom.
Solution: The matcha base was not whisked enough. Whisk until fully dissolved and slightly frothy before adding milk or ice.

Problem: Drink tastes weak or watery.
Solution: Use 2g of matcha per serving. Do not add too much milk or water. The matcha-to-liquid ratio matters.

Problem: Matcha has turned brown or yellow.
Solution: The matcha has oxidized. Check storage conditions — keep in airtight container in refrigerator. Use within 2-4 weeks of opening.

Storage for Cafe Environment:
- Keep opened matcha in the refrigerator in an airtight, opaque container.
- Take out only what you need for service and return the container promptly.
- Use opened matcha within 2-4 weeks.
- Unopened matcha can last up to 1 year when stored in a cool, dark place.
- Never leave matcha exposed to air, light, or heat for extended periods.

Customer Questions Baristas Should Know:

Q: What makes NAKAI matcha different?
A: NAKAI is 100% organic, JAS-certified, sourced from Japan's finest tea farms. Our matcha is curated by tea ceremony and Zen masters, stone-ground for maximum freshness and nutrient preservation.

Q: What is the difference between REVI and IKIGAI?
A: REVI is SS Grade Plus — our highest quality, with deep umami and a creamy, elegant profile. Perfect for drinking straight or as a premium latte. IKIGAI is SS Grade — a versatile premium matcha great for everyday drinking and lattes.

Q: Does matcha have caffeine?
A: Yes, about 68mg per 2g serving (similar to a cup of green tea, less than coffee). But matcha also contains L-theanine, which provides calm, sustained energy without the jitters or crash of coffee.

Q: Is matcha good for you?
A: Matcha is rich in antioxidants (EGCG), L-theanine for calm focus, and has been associated with improved metabolism, heart health, and mental clarity.

Q: Can I make matcha without a whisk?
A: A bamboo chasen is ideal, but you can use a small electric milk frother, a cocktail shaker with ice, or even a jar with a tight lid for shaking.
